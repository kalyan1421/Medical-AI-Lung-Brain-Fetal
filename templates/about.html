Dataset Selection & Rationale

Why we chose these specific datasets for each medical condition

ğŸ« Pneumonia Dataset - 5,856 Chest X-Rays

Source: Guangzhou Women and Children's Medical Center

Why This Dataset? Expert-validated by board-certified radiologists with multiple verification rounds. Includes both bacterial and viral pneumonia cases from real clinical settings. The class imbalance (2.89:1 pneumonia:normal) reflects real-world diagnostic scenarios where positive cases are more common.

Clinical Validation: Images were screened for quality control, removing low-quality scans. All diagnoses were confirmed by at least two independent radiologists, ensuring ground truth accuracy.

ğŸ§  Brain Tumor Dataset - 7,023 MRI Scans

Source: Figshare, SARTAJ, Medical Imaging Repositories

Why This Dataset? Perfectly balanced across 4 classes (Glioma, Meningioma, No Tumor, Pituitary) preventing model bias. Collected from multiple medical centers ensuring diversity. All tumors confirmed through biopsy or surgical pathology (gold standard).

Standardization: MRI sequences (T1, T2, FLAIR) are normalized for consistent contrast and resolution. Expert neurosurgeons annotated each image, making this dataset clinical-grade for AI training.

ğŸ‘¶ Fetal Ultrasound Dataset - 999 HC18 Images

Source: HC18 Grand Challenge (Medical Imaging)

Why This Dataset? International standard benchmark used in medical AI competitions. Pixel-perfect manual segmentations by experienced sonographers. Covers gestational ages 14-40 weeks, representing full pregnancy range. Used in clinical research worldwide.

Quality Control: Each ultrasound verified by multiple experts. Ground truth masks drawn following clinical protocols for head circumference measurement. Images include varying quality levels (shadows, artifacts) matching real clinical conditions.
